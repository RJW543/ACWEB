Curatorial Statement

About this work
Spirit and Teeth
Juan-Miguel Guimarães
A visceral meditation on ancestral memory and bodily inheritance. Guimarães layers organic pigments with found textile fragments — linen sourced from his grandmother's workshop in Porto — creating surfaces that appear to breathe, decay, and regenerate simultaneously. The mask motif recurs throughout his practice as a threshold between the visible self and the inherited other.

About this work
Membrane Study No. 7
Elija Craigl
Rogers' ongoing Membrane series investigates the liminal spaces between cellular biology and architectural form. Working with oil on raw aluminium, each piece captures the moment a boundary becomes permeable — when inside and outside lose their meaning. The reflective substrate creates a shifting dialogue between pigment and viewer, ensuring no two encounters with the work are identical.

About this work
Meat (or How to Kill Oedipus in Cyberspace)
N.E. Rogers
Craigl's photographic practice dismantles the authority of the gaze. Shot on large-format film in abandoned institutional spaces across the American Midwest, each image is then subjected to chemical interference — bleaching, re-exposing, layering — until the photograph forgets what it was meant to document. What remains is the residue of attention itself.
